Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about our dermatology services

Dermatologists diagnose and treat over 3,000 different conditions affecting the skin, hair, and nails. Common conditions include acne, eczema, psoriasis, rosacea, skin cancer, warts, fungal infections, hair loss, and nail disorders. We also provide cosmetic treatments for aging skin, scars, and unwanted hair.

For general skin health, we recommend an annual skin check, especially if you have a history of sun exposure or skin cancer. Those with chronic conditions like acne or psoriasis may need visits every few months. Cosmetic treatment schedules vary based on the procedure. Our dermatologist will recommend a personalized follow-up plan during your first visit.

Your first visit will include a thorough medical history review and skin examination. For medical concerns, we may perform diagnostic tests like biopsies. For cosmetic consultations, we'll discuss your goals and recommend appropriate treatments. Please arrive 15 minutes early to complete paperwork and bring any relevant medical records or current medications.

We accept most major insurance plans for medical dermatology services. Cosmetic procedures are typically not covered by insurance. Our staff will verify your benefits before your visit and inform you of any expected copays or deductibles. Please bring your insurance card and photo ID to your appointment.

Preparation depends on the specific treatment. Generally, avoid sun exposure, retinoids, and blood-thinning medications before procedures. For injectables, we recommend avoiding alcohol 24 hours prior. For laser treatments, discontinue tanning 4 weeks before. You'll receive detailed pre-care instructions during your consultation to ensure optimal results and safety.
